How it works

The year runs in 52 weeks across four seasons. Every week brings a decision. Where you ranch decides your weather, costs, and which markets you can reach. You start small — just you, a horse, a few head of cattle and a barn — and grow from there. Money is real: cattle are priced by the hundredweight (cwt), feed and weather will eat you alive if you aren't paying attention.

Choose your operation

Two classic ways to run cattle. This is the biggest decision you'll make.

🐮 Cow-Calf

You keep a permanent breeding herd of mama cows and a bull. Each spring the cows calve, you raise the calves on grass, then wean and sell them in the fall as feeder calves.

Feel: steady, grass-based, lower feed bills, slow money. The bedrock of the beef business.

🥩 Finisher (Feedlot)

You buy feeder cattle from cow-calf ranchers, then feed them a grain ration to put on weight fast and finish them to slaughter weight, selling fed cattle to packers.

Feel: fast cycles (~5 months), big feed bills, higher risk/reward, swings hard with corn & beef prices.
